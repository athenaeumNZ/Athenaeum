Track Crate ID's column:

Every track on a release is sub-categorized to make it easier to find tunes you are into. The Crate ID's column is where you will find this. I have dubbed this system, 'Maxwell Coding'.

Firstly, tracks are given a genre and sometimes a sub genre i.e., 'Dnb', 'DnB Minimal', 'DnB Liquid', 'Techno Deep' etc.. The sub categories will generally be actual sub genres. However, sometimes the sub category may be more about describing a characteristic of the track. E.g., 'DnB Minimal' is saying that the track is more stripped back rather than saying the sub genre is Minimal DnB.

Secondly, you will see a letter followed by a number at the end of the crate ID. The letter tells you the vibe of the tune. I have found that there isn't really a word that can describe the vibe of a tune. Instead, I use colours to generalize a vibe. Blue tunes are clinical and/or tech styled and are generally a bit on the dark side. Think Bad Company, Stakka & Skynet, Kemal, Dispatch Recordings. Red tunes generally use breaks as a base for their drum loops. Think old school DnB, amens, Metalheadz, Ray Keith, Cylon Recordings.

Yellow tunes are tracks that are palatable to a wider audience. Nothing sinister, just good vibes but not really on the commercial side of things. Think Dillinja, Advanced Recordings, DJ Rap, Icicle. Green tracks stirs deeper emotions. Think Klute, atmospheric jungle, deep liquid dnb. Pink tunes have a mainstream aspect to them. Think modern Ram Records, DJ Fresh, some Hospital Recordings.

Lastly, track heaviness is scaled from 1 to 5. 1 is the lightest tracks that can be found in that given genre & 5 is the heaviest. Occasionally a track may be given a value of 6 which indicates that tune is really really hard.

On some occasions I wont know how to categorize the tune. In such a case you might see something like 'G1'. That would mean that I don't know what to call the genre but I know the vibe is 'Green' and the energy level is '1'. Don't just accept that the Crate ID categorization is exact, the Crate ID's are simply a tool for making it easier for you to find the tunes you want. {{ library }} does not take any responsibility for the records you choose to order, make sure you listen to the tunes before choosing to purchase them.
